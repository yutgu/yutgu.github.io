Hello, welcome :)

I am a fourth-year PhD candidate working with Xin Xie in the Department of Language Science at the University of California, Irvine.

My research asks how linguistic experience shapes the plasticity of human speech and language processing. As listeners, we constantly encounter variation — regional dialects, nonnative accents, unfamiliar voices — and each encounter reshapes how we process speech. But what kind of learning underlies this flexibility? Does exposure change how we represent sounds at a sensory level, or does it shift the higher-level strategies we use to interpret them? I pursue this question along two converging lines. First, I run behavioral experiments that disentangle the cognitive mechanisms of accent adaptation — teasing apart changes in perceptual representations from shifts in decision-making strategies. Second, I develop new measurement tools to capture the structure of listeners' real-world linguistic experiences, moving beyond static categories toward dynamic, continuous quantification of everyday input. Learn more about my research here!

Recent update: Our paper "The Accent Atlas: A Geolocation-based Assessment of Non-Native Accent Familiarity and Linguistic Diversity" has been published in Psychonomic Bulletin & Review — Hoorah!
